equivalent to the thing sold: and so it not only measures the value of the

But this it does only by its quantity, and nothing else.“ (p. 92.) “The raising
being but giving of names at pleasure to aliquot parts of any piece, viz. that
now the sixtieth part of an ounce shall be called a penny, may be done with
what increase you please.” (118.) “The privilege that bullion has, to be
exported freely, will give it a little advance in price above our coin, let the
denomination of that be raised, or fallen as you please, whilst there is need
of its exportation, and the exportation of our coin is prohibited by law.”
(p. 119, 120.)
