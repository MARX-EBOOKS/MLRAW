cotton required for the weft is provided by speculators, or the whole
amount of cotton required is given to master-weavers, who act as inter-
mediate agents between the speculators and the peasants. The orders
given in the villages by some of these capitalists amount to from 100,000
to 200,000 pieces annually. In some villages they have established large
sheds with looms for the better description of cloth they require, besides
the village looms which they employ.“ (87 l.c.)

Indem die kapitalistische Produktion die Scheidung zwischen Manu-
faktur u. Agrikultur vollzieht, wird der Landarbeiter immer mehr v.

Verschiedenheiten im Umschlag aus; für den Arbeiter nicht. (Z.B. auch
Fischfang, dessen Arbeitsperiode an bestimmte seasons gebunden ist.)
