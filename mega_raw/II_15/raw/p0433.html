demand, may be divided into two branches, corresponding with the distinction pointed out

The other branch is to receive deposits of the incomes of their customers, and to pay out the
amount, as it is wanted for expenditure by the latter in the objects of their consumption …
the former being a circulation of capital, the latter of currency.” – Tooke, Inquiry into the
Currency Principle, p. 36. Das erstere ist „the concentration of capital on the one hand and
the distribution of it on the other, das zweite ist administering the circulation for local

Kapitalbetrags in Geld für einen gleichen Kapitalbetrag in Waaren. Aber Geld ausgelegt in
