“The natural price of any article, is that … bestowed upon its production … Its (labour's)
natural price … consists, in such a quantity of the necessaries and comforts of life, as, from
the nature of the climate, and the habits of the country, are necessary to support the labourer,
and to enable him to rear such a family as may preserve, in the market, an undiminished sup-
ply of labour … The natural price of labour … though it varies under different climates, and
with the different stages of national improvement, may, in any given time and place be re-
garded as very nearly stationary.” (R. Torrens: An Essay on the External Corn Trade. London
1815. p. 55-65 passim.)
