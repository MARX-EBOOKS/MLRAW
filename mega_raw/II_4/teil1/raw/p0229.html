b) Ricardo, bei Betrachtung des auswärtigen Handels, nimmt keine Rücksicht auf Erweiterung
der Productivkraft des angewandten Capitals durch Erweiterung des Markts. Daß im übrigen
die costs of transport etc ganz wirken wie alle andren Circulationskosten, d. h. Erhöhung des
Werths wie dieß geschieht durch rendering the labor employed more unproductive, hebt er
richtig hervor, ohne daher den richtigen Schluß zu ziehn, daß das whole mercantile capital
belongs to the faux frais de production, necessitated by the present system.
Er citirt von dem faden Say: “Commerce enables us to obtain a commodity in the place
where it is to be found, and to convey it to another where it is to be consumed; it therefore
gives us the power of increasing the value of the commodity, by the whole difference between
its price in the first of these places, and its price in the second.” Und er bemerkt dazu: “True,
but how is this additional value given to it? By adding to the cost of production, first, the ex-
penses of conveyance; secondly, the profit on the advances of capital made by the merchant.
The commodity is only more valuable, for the same reasons that every other commodity may
become more valuable, because more labor is expended on its production and conveyance, before
it is purchased by the consumer. This must not be mentioned as one of the advantages of
commerce. When the subject is more closely examined, it will be found that the whole profit
of commerce resolve themselves into the means which it gives us of acquiring, not more valu-
able objects, but more useful ones.” (Ricardo. Principles of Political Economy. Note. p. 309,
310.)
