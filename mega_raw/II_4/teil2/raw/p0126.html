“One good effect of legislation upon the subject of the fencing of machinery
is to be found in the fact, that no new machines ever leave the premises of
the engineer now, in which all outside wheels are not ‘well and securely
fenced’ by iron casings; the ends of the old frames are a mass of complicated
wheels, in the new ones not a wheel can be touched, nor in many frames can
one even be seen; and I trust the time is not far distant when an engineer
will as rarely erect mill-gearing which he has not ‘well and securely fenced’,
by some appropriate and sufficient means.” (l. c. p. 56)
