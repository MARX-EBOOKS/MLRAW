Aërated Water. “In aërated water making, the sulphuric acid is simply used to
generate carbonic acid gas from carbonate of lime in a separate and distinct
vessel, … the gas so generated is received into a gas holder for use, whilst
he sulphate of lime formed in the generator, in which any arsenic that might
possibly be in the sulphuric acid is retained, is afterwards thrown away or
used for making (artificial) manure.” (Dr. Dauglish)
