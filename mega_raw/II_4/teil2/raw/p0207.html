“Although the Indian cotton may (in einigen mills) have been worked to

Surat be confirmed, the operatives will want to earn the wages of 1861,
which would seriously affect the profits of the manufacturer, unless he obtain
compensation either in the price of the raw cotton or of his products.”170)

House rent und Emigration. “The rent is frequently deducted from the
wages of operatives, even when working short time, by the manufacturers whose
cottages they may be occupying. Nevertheless the value of this class of pro-
perty has diminished, and houses may be obtained at a reduction of from
25 to 50 % upon the rent of the houses in ordinary times; f. i. a cottage
which would have cost 3 s. 6 d. per week can now be had for 2 s. 4 d. per
week, and sometimes even for less.”171)
