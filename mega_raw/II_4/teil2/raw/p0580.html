897 “The Export Trade (for India and China) has, for some time past,
principally been carried on for account of the manufacturers; and, in order
to give time for the returns from the East, the Merchants who have been
the principal parties carrying on that trade have given their acceptances for
short periods, – 3 months or 6 months –, and with an understanding that
the bills, when they fell due, should be renewed for a further period, so as
to give time for the return of the proceed from India. That was the
course … when Money Pressure … in the Autumn of 1847. The Banks,
from the Discredit then existing, refused to renew the Description of Bills
referred to, which threw the advance upon many of the exporting mer-
chants, whose capital were not equal to sustain the Demand upon them. I
believe such to have been the principal cause of the East India Failures.”
