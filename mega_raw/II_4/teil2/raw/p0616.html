1344 (Quantity of Money) “We never apply to the Bank, unless the
amount of circulating medium in the City is below the Requirements, then
we became in a large degree the Agents in getting it from the Bank. If the
supply of Circulating Medium is adequate we never go near them.”

1514 (Loyd) “Pressure, and a high rate of interest, caused by the want of
sufficient capital, cannot be relieved by an extra Issue of Bank notes.”

1588 (Idem) “Credit is a Means of obtaining another Man's Capital.”

1589 (Idem) “An Interruption to Credit is an Interruption to the Facility
of so borrowing other people's Capital to carry on Trade.”

1604 (Idem) “in fact the high Rate of Interest and the Depression of the
manufacturing interests was the necessary result of the Diminution of the
national Capital, applicable to manufacturing and trading Purposes.” (Er
schwärmt natürlich für high Interest). |

1665. (Idem) “it still had the effect of causing a greater importation of

1709. (Idem) “Under circumstances of Great Pressure upon the Country,
it (the Bank of England) commands the Rate of Interest.” 1710 Whenever
the discounts of the private bankers or brokers become comparatively lim-
ited, they fall upon the Bank of England, and then it … has the power of
commanding the Market Rate.

1721. (Idem) (October 1847) “there was an actual hoarding on the Part of

1729 (Idem) “It (the Power of the Bank of making advances) did not pre-
vent the failure of houses which ought to have failed, nor will it ever, unless
they make advances upon improper securities. But the fear in October (47)
was not for insolvent but for solvent houses.”

1736 (Idem) “It (the government letter) produced the same effect as if the
