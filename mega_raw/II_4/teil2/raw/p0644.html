the iron furnaces was 33500 tons, in 1840 about 250000 tons. In 1826 the

with all this progress in industry and wealth, the circulation of notes which
in 1825 varied from 3400000 l. to 4700000 was in 1829 von 2960000 l. to
3670000 und in den ersten 3 Monaten von 1840: £ 2940000.‘“

“The country circulation is found to decrease and expand in correspon-
dence with the amount of business transacted at different periods of the
year. In this respect its variations are of a uniform and general character. It
decreases half a million every year from spring to the summer quarterly
and expands again by degrees towards autumn and Christmas etc.” (Aus-
sage von H. W. Hobhouse.) (banker at Bath.) („Evidence on Banks of Issue“.
Select Committee of the H. o. C. in 1841.)
