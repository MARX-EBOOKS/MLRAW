“One great drawback attends cereals generally … they exhaust the soil

ments of their growth, while they give to the soil more than they take from it;
thus both directly, and by their conversion into animal manure, contribut-
ing in two ways to repair the mischief done by cereals and exhausting crops
generally; one principle, therefore, is that they should at least alternate with
these crops; in this consists the Norfolk rotation.” (50, 51.)
